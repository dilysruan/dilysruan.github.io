Research

Here's a list of my publications on ADS and here are some of my selected publications:

  1. "Statistical Bias in the Hubble Constant and Mass Power Law Slope for Mock Strong Lenses" D. Ruan , C. Keeton, in prep
  2. "Discovery of a pulsar wind nebula around B0950 + 08 with the ELWA" D. Ruan , G. B. Taylor, J. Dowell, K. Stovall, F. K. Schinzel, P. B. Demorest, 2020, MNRAS, arXiv:2005.03303


I'm interested in exploring cosmology questions (i.e. Hubble tension, dark matter) through the tool of gravitational lensing and hydrodynamic cosmological simulations. I have experience working with pygravlens, lensmodel, emcee, gala, and simulations like IllustrisTNG and Romulus25.

Currently I'm working with Chuck Keeton, focusing on lens models and physically-motivated priors to improve measurements of the Hubble constant through strongly lensed quasars by massive elliptical galaxies. I'm also interested in learning the nature of dark matter through small scales, which can be done through strong lensing and weak lensing from dwarf galaxies. I am a member of the LSST Strong Lensing Science Collaboration and the Merian Survey. In undergrad, I studied radio pulsars using the Expanded Long Wavelength Array with Dr. Greg Taylor. I also compared deconvolution and masking algorithms in radio synthesis imaging with Kumar Golap and Takahiro Tsutsumi during the NRAO REU program.

I am second-generation Chinese American. I was born and raised in Albuquerque, New Mexico, where I enjoyed sunny desert days and expansive horizons. Now I'm enjoying the urban clusters and forest-y pockets of the East Coast. I also have a pet turtle named Toph.
I believe it is vital to make physics and higher education more accessible, equitable, and diverse. I am a member of the Rutgers Minorities in Physics and Astronomy (MiPA) group and serve as the co-organizer of our Equity and Inclusion Journal Club.
