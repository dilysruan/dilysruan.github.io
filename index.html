Research

Broadly, I am interested in exploring cosmology questions (the Hubble tension, the nature of dark matter, how structure formed in the early universe) through the tool of gravitational lensing and hydrodynamic cosmological simulations.

Here's a list of my publications on ADS.
Currently I focus on free-form lens models (modelling the total mass distribution with flexible models rather than strictly parametric) to improve strong lensing measurements of the Hubble constant. I'm also interested in learning the nature of dark matter through small scales, which we can do through strong lensing and the weak lensing signal from dwarf galaxies. My advisor is Dr. Chuck Keeton.
I have experience with pygravlens, lensmodel, emcee, and data from simulations like IllustrisTNG.
Previously I studied radio pulsars using the Expanded Long Wavelength Array with Dr. Greg Taylor. I also compared deconvolution and masking algorithms in radio synthesis imaging with Dr. Kumar Golap and Dr. Takahiro Tsutsumi during the NRAO REU program.
