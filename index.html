Research

I'm interested in exploring cosmology questions (i.e. Hubble tension, dark matter) through the tool of gravitational lensing and hydrodynamic cosmological simulations. I've worked with pygravlens, lensmodel, halotools, gala, emcee, and simulations like IllustrisTNG and Romulus.

My advisor is Chuck Keeton, and we focus on lens models and physically-motivated priors to improve measurements of the Hubble constant through strongly lensed quasars by massive elliptical galaxies. I'm also working with Alyson Brooks to learn the nature of dark matter through small scales, which can be done through the weak lensing - HI gas kinematics relation from dwarf galaxies. I am a member of the LSST Strong Lensing Science Collaboration and the Merian Survey.

Here's a list of my publications on ADS and here are some of my selected publications:

  1. "Statistical Bias in the Hubble Constant and Mass Power Law Slope for Mock Strong Lenses" Dilys Ruan, Charles R. Keeton, 2023, submitted to MNRAS
  2. "Discovery of a pulsar wind nebula around B0950 + 08 with the ELWA" D. Ruan, G. B. Taylor, J. Dowell, K. Stovall, F. K. Schinzel, P. B. Demorest, 2020, MNRAS, arXiv:2005.03303

In undergrad, I studied radio pulsars using the Expanded Long Wavelength Array with Greg Taylor. I also compared deconvolution and masking algorithms in radio synthesis imaging with Kumar Golap and Takahiro Tsutsumi during the NRAO REU program.

Dilys Ruan ("De"-"lise" "Roo"-"awn") (阮嘉乐) (she/her)
I am second-generation Chinese American. I was born and raised in Albuquerque, New Mexico, where I enjoyed sunny desert days and expansive horizons. I also have a pet turtle named Toph!
I believe it is vital to make physics and higher education more accessible, equitable, and diverse. I am a member of the Rutgers Minorities in Physics and Astronomy (MiPA) group. I am also the co-president of the Rutgers Physics and Astronomy Graduate Student Organization (GSO).
