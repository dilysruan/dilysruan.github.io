Research

I am interested in answering cosmology questions (dark energy, dark matter, galaxy formation) through the tool of gravitational lensing. Currently I am studying how to improve measurements of the Hubble constant through free-form lens models of galaxy-galaxy strong lensing. My advisor is Dr. Chuck Keeton. I am a member of the LSST Strong Lensing Science Collaboration.
In undergrad, I studied radio pulsars using the Expanded Long Wavelength Array with the advisement of Dr. Greg Taylor. Before that, I compared deconvolution and masking algorithms in radio synthesis imaging with Dr. Kumar Golap and Dr. Takahiro Tsutsumi during my REU program.

About Me

My first name is pronounced like "D"+"Elise" and my last name is pronounced like "Roo"+"awn". I use she/her pronouns.
I believe it is vital to make physics and higher education more accessible, equitable, and diverse. I am a member of the Rutgers Minorities in Physics and Astronomy (MiPA) group and serve as the co-organizer of our Equity and Inclusion Journal Club.
I was born and raised in Albuquerque, New Mexico (the 505!). I am a second-generation Chinese American. My parents immigrated from Guangzhou, China in the 1980s and opened a restaurant in the US. I really admire my family and am so grateful for them.
